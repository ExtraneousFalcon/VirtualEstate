    Investing 101


    Market Volatility
    From our research, we concluded three main factors play into how volatile the housing market is at a given time:

    The first factor being the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for housing in U.S. cities which measures for change in the prices that urban consumers pay for real estate. The CPI accounts and tracks inflation which helps investors rationalize their prices for purchasing a home and basing their rent price as a tenant.

    Another factor that plays into market volatility is the Housing Affordability Index (HAI) which measures if a family making the median income can afford a median-priced home. For example, the HAI is over a 100 that indicates that a family has a surplus of income to purchase a median priced home. This metric helps dictate the demand of homeowners in the current market.

    The last factor important for measuring volatility is the Interest Rate as lower interest rates allow home buyers to purchase homes with a lower mortgage. Allowing home buyers to purchase more expensive homes leads to a higher demand for real estate further making home prices go up.

    Investment Factors
    One simple factor used to take into account when purchasing a new real estate investment is the Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM) which calculates the purchase price/ the total annual rent. A lower GRM reflects a healthier market.

    Another basic factor is the One Percent Rule (OPR) which is the rule of thumb that the monthly gross rent should be more than or equal to 1% of the total purchase price. This can indicate how considerable a property's rental income may be.

    A more accurate factor for investments is the Cap Rate which calculates the net operating income annually divided by the total purchase price. For our estimation we use a standard variation of the taxes and fees expected in the desired area.

    Another important factor for purchasing a home is its Net Income After Financing (NIAF) which is the net operating costs annually minus the financing cost. This figure estimates how much money you directly put in the bank. For our estimation we use a standard variation of the taxes and fees expected in the desired area.

    Finally, the factor Cash on Cash Return (COCR) calculates the NIAF divided by the down payment of the home. For our figures account 20% of the purchase price as the calculation of the down payment.
